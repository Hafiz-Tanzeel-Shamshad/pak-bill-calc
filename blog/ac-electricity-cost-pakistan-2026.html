AC Electricity Cost in Pakistan 2026: Complete Breakdown

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

Why ACs Drive Up Your Bill

Air conditioners are the single largest electricity consumer in most Pakistani households during summer. A single AC running 8-10 hours per night can double or triple your monthly bill. Understanding exactly how much your AC costs to run helps you make smarter decisions about usage, temperature settings, and when to upgrade.

This guide breaks down the cost of running different AC types in Pakistan based on NEPRA 2026 tariff rates.

Power Consumption by AC Type

AC power consumption depends on tonnage, inverter technology, and energy efficiency rating. Typical consumption per hour:

  • 1 Ton Non-Inverter: 1.3 to 1.5 kWh per hour
  • 1 Ton Inverter: 0.8 to 1.0 kWh per hour (average)
  • 1.5 Ton Non-Inverter: 1.8 to 2.0 kWh per hour
  • 1.5 Ton Inverter: 1.2 to 1.5 kWh per hour (average)
  • 2 Ton Non-Inverter: 2.3 to 2.8 kWh per hour

Inverter ACs use 30-40% less power because they do not cycle on and off. They run continuously at a lower speed, which is more efficient.

Monthly Cost at NEPRA 2026 Rates

Assume 8 hours of daily use for 30 days at an average rate of Rs. 42 per unit (301-400 unit slab):

  • 1 Ton Non-Inverter (1.4 kWh): 1.4 × 8 × 30 = 336 units. Cost: Rs. 14,112
  • 1 Ton Inverter (0.9 kWh): 0.9 × 8 × 30 = 216 units. Cost: Rs. 9,072
  • 1.5 Ton Non-Inverter (1.9 kWh): 1.9 × 8 × 30 = 456 units. Cost: Rs. 19,152
  • 1.5 Ton Inverter (1.35 kWh): 1.35 × 8 × 30 = 324 units. Cost: Rs. 13,608

Running a 1.5 ton non-inverter AC for 8 hours daily adds nearly Rs. 20,000 to your monthly bill. Switching to an inverter AC saves Rs. 5,000-6,000 per month.

Impact of Temperature Setting

Lower temperature settings increase power consumption significantly:

  • 18-20°C: AC runs near full power constantly (highest cost)
  • 22-24°C: Moderate power, compressor cycles reasonably
  • 26°C: Optimal balance of comfort and efficiency
  • 28°C: Lowest power use but may feel warm in peak summer

Each degree below 26°C increases power consumption by approximately 6%. Running an AC at 20°C instead of 26°C can increase the bill by 30-40%.

Tips to Reduce AC Electricity Cost

Follow these practical tips to lower your AC bill:

  • Set thermostat to 26°C for the best balance of comfort and savings
  • Use ceiling fans with the AC to distribute cool air and allow higher temperature settings
  • Close doors and windows in the room being cooled
  • Clean AC filters every 2 weeks during summer for maximum efficiency
  • Service the AC before summer to check gas pressure and coil cleanliness
  • Use curtains or blinds to block direct sunlight during the day
  • Consider an inverter AC if your current unit is over 5 years old

Comparing AC vs Other Appliances

To put AC costs in perspective, a 1.5 ton AC running 8 hours uses more electricity than all other home appliances combined. A refrigerator uses about 120 units per month, a ceiling fan uses about 60 units, and LED lights use 15-20 units total. The AC alone can consume 300-500 units in summer, which often pushes households into higher slab rates.

FAQs

How much does an AC add to my electricity bill?

A 1.5 ton non-inverter AC running 8 hours daily adds approximately Rs. 19,000-23,000 per month depending on your slab rate. An inverter AC adds about Rs. 13,000-16,000.

Does inverter AC really save money?

Yes. Inverter ACs use 30-40% less power than non-inverter models, saving Rs. 5,000-7,000 per month during summer. The payback period is typically 2-3 seasons.

What AC temperature is most economical?

26°C is the most cost-effective temperature. Each degree lower increases power use by about 6%.

How can I calculate my AC cost?

Multiply your AC's kWh rating by hours of use, then by per-unit rate. Use the Pakistan electricity bill calculator on this site for an accurate estimate.

Does AC maintenance affect electricity consumption?

Yes. Dirty filters, low gas, and dusty coils increase power consumption by 15-30%. Regular servicing before summer is essential.
