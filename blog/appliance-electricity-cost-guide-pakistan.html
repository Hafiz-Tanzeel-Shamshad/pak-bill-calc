Appliance Electricity Cost Guide 2026: What Each Appliance Costs to Run

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

Why Appliance Costs Matter

Knowing the electricity cost of each appliance helps you identify where your bill money goes and which devices to upgrade first. In Pakistan, a few high-consumption appliances (AC, water pump, geyser) can account for 70-80% of your total bill, while many small devices contribute very little.

This guide ranks household appliances by their monthly electricity cost so you can prioritize energy-saving measures effectively.

Cost per Hour at NEPRA 2026 Rates

Below is the cost of running each appliance for one hour at the 301-400 unit slab rate (Rs. 42/unit). Your actual rate may vary based on total monthly consumption. Use our bill calculator for an exact estimate:

  • 1.5 Ton Non-Inverter AC: 1.9 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 80/hour
  • 1.5 Ton Inverter AC: 1.35 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 57/hour
  • 1 Ton Non-Inverter AC: 1.4 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 59/hour
  • Electric Geyser (1500W): 1.5 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 63/hour
  • Electric Iron (1200W): 1.2 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 50/hour
  • Water Pump 1 HP (746W): 0.746 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 31/hour
  • Microwave (1000W): 1 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 42/hour
  • Washing Machine (500W): 0.5 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 21/hour
  • Refrigerator (200W avg): 0.2 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 8.40/hour (runs ~9 hrs/day = Rs. 76/day)
  • Ceiling Fan (80W): 0.08 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 3.36/hour
  • LED TV 32" (60W): 0.06 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 2.52/hour
  • LED Bulb (10W): 0.01 kWh × Rs. 42 = Rs. 0.42/hour

Monthly Cost Comparison (8 Hours Daily)

For appliances typically used 8 hours per day (30 days):

  • 1.5 Ton Non-Inverter AC: Rs. 19,152/month
  • 1.5 Ton Inverter AC: Rs. 13,608/month
  • Ceiling Fan: Rs. 806/month
  • LED TV: Rs. 605/month
  • LED Bulb (10 of them): Rs. 1,008/month

The difference is striking: one AC costs more than 20 fans or 15 LED TVs. If you need to reduce your bill, AC usage is where the biggest savings are. See our bill reduction guide for detailed strategies.

Appliances with the Most Savings Potential

These upgrades offer the fastest payback:

  • Replace AC with inverter model: Save Rs. 5,000-6,000/month in summer
  • Replace old bulbs with LEDs: Save Rs. 500-4,000/month depending on current bulb type
  • Replace old refrigerator: New energy-efficient fridges use 40-50% less power
  • Use solar water heater instead of electric geyser: Save Rs. 500-2,000/month in winter

For a detailed comparison of bulb costs, read our LED vs old bulbs guide.

Appliance Cost vs Slab Rate Impact

An important factor in Pakistan is that adding a high-consumption appliance can push you into a higher slab rate, increasing the cost per unit for all your electricity usage — not just for that appliance. For example, if you consume 250 units and add an AC that uses 300 units, your total becomes 550 units. The rate on all 550 units jumps from the protected slab (~Rs. 12/unit) to the 301-400 slab (~Rs. 42/unit).

This means the actual cost of running that AC is much higher than just its unit consumption multiplied by the per-unit rate. Use the bill calculator to model this effect accurately.

FAQs

Which appliance uses the most electricity in a Pakistani home?

The air conditioner, especially non-inverter models. A 1.5 ton AC uses more electricity than all other appliances combined in summer.

How much does it cost to run a ceiling fan per month?

A ceiling fan running 12 hours daily costs approximately Rs. 1,200-1,500 per month depending on the slab rate.

Does a refrigerator consume a lot of electricity?

A typical 12 cu ft refrigerator uses about 120-180 units per month, costing Rs. 5,000-7,500 at average rates. Energy-efficient models use less.

How can I reduce appliance electricity costs?

Use inverter ACs, LED bulbs, and energy-efficient refrigerators. Shift heavy usage to off-peak hours if you have a ToU meter.

What is the most energy-efficient appliance in a home?

LED bulbs are the most efficient — a 10W LED produces the same light as a 60W incandescent bulb while using 83% less power.
