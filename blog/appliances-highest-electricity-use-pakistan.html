Which Appliances Use the Most Electricity in Pakistan? (With Unit Cost)

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

${img}

Knowing which appliances consume the most electricity helps you reduce your monthly bill. In Pakistan, cooling appliances such as air conditioners and water pumps are usually the biggest contributors. This guide lists common appliances, their typical power usage, and how to estimate their cost based on units.

For an overall bill estimate, use the main calculator or your DISCO specific calculator like PESCO. Then compare that with the appliance table below to spot your biggest cost drivers.

How to estimate appliance cost

Estimate the cost by multiplying the units consumed by your average per-unit rate. For example, if your average rate is Rs 30 per unit and your AC uses 1.5 units per hour, one hour of AC costs about Rs 45. If you use it for 6 hours daily, that is about Rs 8,100 per month. These are rough estimates but help you identify which appliance matters most.

Which appliances to focus on

Air conditioners, water pumps, and electric heaters are the biggest drivers. Refrigerators run all day, so replacing an old model with an efficient inverter model can save units over the month. Lighting is less significant, but switching to LEDs is still recommended because the cost is small and the payback is fast.

Use slab awareness to reduce cost

When you reduce appliance usage, you reduce total units and can avoid crossing into higher slabs. This amplifies savings. Use the slab chart to see how a small reduction could keep you in a cheaper bracket.

Consider solar for high usage appliances

Solar can offset heavy appliance usage, especially ACs and pumps. Use the solar savings calculator to estimate system size and payback. For a full analysis, read Is solar worth it in Pakistan?.

FAQs

Which appliance uses the most electricity?

Air conditioners typically consume the most electricity in Pakistani homes.

Are inverter appliances cheaper to run?

Yes, inverter appliances use less power by adjusting speed and avoiding constant on-off cycles.

Does fridge usage matter?

Yes, refrigerators run all day and can add significant monthly units.

How can I estimate appliance cost?

Multiply power rating by hours used, then apply your average per-unit rate.

Can solar reduce appliance cost?

Yes, solar reduces grid units, lowering the effective cost of appliance usage.
