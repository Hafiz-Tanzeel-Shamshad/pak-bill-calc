Electricity Bill Complaint: How to File a Complaint with Your DISCO

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

If your electricity bill seems incorrect, you have the right to file a complaint. Common issues include wrong meter readings, sudden slab jumps, or incorrect customer details. This guide explains when to complain, what documents to prepare, and how to follow up with your DISCO.

Before filing a complaint, estimate your bill using the main calculator and compare it with the official bill. This helps you identify whether the issue is usage related or a billing error.

When should you file a complaint?

You should file a complaint if the unit reading appears incorrect, the bill includes charges you do not recognize, or your name and address are wrong. Also complain if your meter is faulty, there is evidence of tampering, or your bill is unusually high compared to previous months without a clear reason.

Documents to keep ready

Prepare a copy of the latest bill, your CNIC, and a photo of your meter reading if possible. These documents help the DISCO verify your claim quickly. If you have previous bills, bring those too for comparison.

How to file a complaint

  1. Visit your local DISCO office or call the helpline.
  2. Provide your reference number and explain the issue.
  3. Submit documents and request a complaint number.
  4. Follow up within a week if you do not get a response.

Online and app based complaints

Some DISCOs provide online complaint portals or mobile apps. These can be quicker because you receive a digital complaint number. Check your DISCO website or app for availability. If the online system is down, file the complaint in person.

What happens after you file

The DISCO typically assigns a team to verify the meter reading or inspect the meter. If the complaint is valid, they may issue a corrected bill or adjust your account. If the complaint is rejected, ask for a written explanation so you can consider escalation.

Escalation options

If your complaint is not resolved, you can escalate to senior DISCO offices or NEPRA consumer services. Keep all documents and complaint numbers so you can present a clear record.

FAQs

When should I file a bill complaint?

File a complaint if the bill shows an incorrect reading, unexpected charges, or wrong customer details.

What documents do I need?

Keep a copy of the bill, your CNIC, and a photo of the meter reading if possible.

How long does complaint resolution take?

Time varies by DISCO, but follow up within a week if there is no response.

Can I complain online?

Some DISCOs offer online portals or apps for complaints.

What if my complaint is rejected?

Ask for a written explanation and consider escalating to higher offices or NEPRA if needed.
