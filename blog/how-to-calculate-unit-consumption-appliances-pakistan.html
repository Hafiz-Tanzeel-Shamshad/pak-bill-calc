How to Calculate Unit Consumption of Home Appliances in Pakistan

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

What Is a Unit of Electricity?

In Pakistan, electricity is measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh). One unit equals one kilowatt-hour. The formula to calculate units consumed by any appliance is:

Watts × Hours Used ÷ 1000 = Units (kWh)

For example, a 100-watt bulb running for 10 hours consumes 100 × 10 ÷ 1000 = 1 unit of electricity. This simple formula helps you estimate how much each appliance contributes to your monthly bill.

Appliance Wattage Reference Table

Use these typical wattage values for common Pakistani appliances:

  • LED Bulb: 9-12W
  • CFL Bulb: 14-20W
  • Ceiling Fan: 75-100W
  • Pedestal Fan: 50-70W
  • 1 Ton AC (Non-Inverter): 1,300-1,500W
  • 1.5 Ton AC (Non-Inverter): 1,800-2,000W
  • 1 Ton AC (Inverter): 800-1,000W (average)
  • Refrigerator (12 cu ft): 150-250W (runs 8-10 hrs/day)
  • Deep Freezer: 200-350W
  • Water Pump (1 HP): 746W
  • Washing Machine: 400-800W per cycle
  • Electric Iron: 1,000-1,500W
  • Microwave: 800-1,200W
  • TV (32 inch LED): 50-80W
  • Laptop: 45-65W
  • WiFi Router: 10-15W

Step-by-Step Calculation Example

Let us calculate the daily units for a typical Pakistani home:

Room 1:

  • 1 LED bulb (10W) × 6 hours = 60 Wh
  • 1 ceiling fan (80W) × 12 hours = 960 Wh
  • 1.5 ton inverter AC (1,350W avg) × 8 hours = 10,800 Wh

Kitchen:

  • 1 LED bulb (10W) × 4 hours = 40 Wh
  • 1 refrigerator (200W) × 9 hours runtime = 1,800 Wh
  • 1 microwave (1,000W) × 0.5 hours = 500 Wh

Other:

  • 1 TV (60W) × 5 hours = 300 Wh
  • 1 water pump (746W) × 1 hour = 746 Wh
  • 2 LED bulbs (20W total) × 6 hours = 120 Wh

Total daily: 60 + 960 + 10,800 + 40 + 1,800 + 500 + 300 + 746 + 120 = 15,326 Wh = 15.3 units per day × 30 = 459 units per month. Use our bill calculator to convert units to rupees.

How to Find Your Appliance Wattage

There are three ways to find exact wattage:

  1. Check the nameplate: Every appliance has a sticker showing power in watts (W) or amperes (A). If amperes are listed, multiply by 220V: Watts = Amps × 220.
  2. Use a power meter: A plug-in power monitor gives real-time readings for any appliance.
  3. Check the energy label: New appliances display annual kWh consumption on the Pakistan Energy Label.

For the most accurate results, measure actual runtime with a timer or estimate based on your usage patterns.

Tracking Your Total Consumption

You can also calculate total home consumption without checking each appliance:

  1. Read your meter on the first day of the month
  2. Read again on the last day
  3. Subtract: Last reading minus first reading = total monthly units

FAQs

How do I calculate units consumed by an appliance?

Multiply watts by hours used, then divide by 1000. Example: 1500W AC × 8 hours ÷ 1000 = 12 units per day.

What is the formula for electricity unit calculation?

Units = (Watts × Hours) ÷ 1000. Each unit equals 1 kilowatt-hour.

How many units does a 1.5 ton AC use per hour?

A non-inverter 1.5 ton AC uses about 1.8-2.0 units per hour. An inverter AC uses about 1.2-1.5 units per hour on average.

How can I check which appliance uses the most electricity?

Use a plug-in power meter or check the nameplate wattage and multiply by estimated hours of use per day.

What is the average electricity consumption of a Pakistani home?

A typical Pakistani home uses 300-600 units per month. Homes with ACs in summer can use 700-1,500 units.
