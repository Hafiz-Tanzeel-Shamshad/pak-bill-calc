Is Solar Worth It in Pakistan? Complete 2026 Cost vs Savings Analysis

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

Solar has moved from a luxury to a serious money saver for many Pakistani households. Rising electricity tariffs and slab rates mean that a well sized solar system can reduce monthly bills dramatically. But is it worth the upfront cost in 2026? This guide breaks down system size, cost, savings, and payback, and shows when solar makes sense for your home.

For a quick estimate, use the solar savings calculator. It takes your monthly units and roof space and gives you a recommended system size and payback period.

How solar savings work

Solar reduces the number of units you buy from the grid. If you consume 400 units and solar provides 200 units, your grid consumption drops to 200 units. This not only lowers the bill, it can keep you in a cheaper slab. The slab effect is critical because the highest slabs are the most expensive. This is why solar often pays back faster for high usage households.

System size and cost in 2026

Most residential systems range from 3 kW to 10 kW. A 5 kW system can produce roughly 600 units per month in good conditions. Costs vary by inverter type, panel quality, and installer. It is best to request multiple quotes and compare warranties, after sales support, and net metering experience.

Payback period

Payback depends on your usage and the slab you are currently in. If you are a non-protected consumer above 300 units, payback can be as low as 3 to 4 years. For lower usage households, payback may be longer. Use the main bill calculator to estimate your current bill and then compare it with the solar estimate to see your savings.

Net metering impact

Net metering allows you to export excess units and get credits. This can increase your savings and shorten payback. Net metering rules vary by DISCO, but most major DISCOs support it. Read the net metering guide for the 2026 process and eligibility.

When solar is worth it

Solar is most worth it if you:

  • Use more than 250 units per month
  • Have enough roof space for panels
  • Can afford the upfront cost or financing
  • Plan to stay in the same home for several years

If you meet these conditions, solar usually pays back within a few years and protects you from future tariff increases.

What about maintenance?

Solar systems require basic cleaning and occasional inverter checks. Dust can reduce output, so cleaning panels every few weeks can keep performance high. Ask your installer for a maintenance plan and warranty details.
