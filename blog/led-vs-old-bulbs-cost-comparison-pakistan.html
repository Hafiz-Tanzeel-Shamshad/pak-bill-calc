LED vs Old Bulbs: Complete Cost Comparison for Pakistan (2026)

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

Why Bulb Type Matters for Your Electricity Bill

Lighting makes up a significant portion of household electricity usage in Pakistan. Many homes still use old incandescent or CFL bulbs that consume far more power than modern LEDs. Replacing them is one of the cheapest ways to reduce monthly units and lower your electricity bill.

This guide compares LED bulbs with incandescent and CFL bulbs on power consumption, cost per month, lifespan, and payback time. Use the figures below to estimate how much you can save by switching all bulbs in your home to LEDs.

Power Consumption Comparison

The table below shows the typical wattage of each bulb type for the same brightness level (measured in lumens):

  • Incandescent: 60W for 800 lumens (standard room brightness)
  • CFL: 14W for 800 lumens
  • LED: 9W for 800 lumens

An LED uses about 85% less power than an incandescent bulb and 35% less than a CFL to produce the same light. For a home with 10 bulbs running 6 hours per day, the difference adds up quickly.

Monthly Cost Calculation

Assume 10 bulbs, 6 hours daily use, and a NEPRA rate of Rs. 42 per unit (average for 301-400 unit slab):

  • Incandescent (60W each): 10 × 60W × 6h = 3,600 Wh = 3.6 units/day × 30 = 108 units/month. Cost: 108 × 42 = Rs. 4,536
  • CFL (14W each): 10 × 14W × 6h = 840 Wh = 0.84 units/day × 30 = 25.2 units/month. Cost: 25.2 × 42 = Rs. 1,058
  • LED (9W each): 10 × 9W × 6h = 540 Wh = 0.54 units/day × 30 = 16.2 units/month. Cost: 16.2 × 42 = Rs. 680

Switching from incandescent to LED saves approximately Rs. 3,856 per month on lighting alone. Even switching from CFL to LED saves about Rs. 378 per month.

Payback Period

LED bulbs cost more upfront than incandescent or CFL bulbs but last much longer and use far less power:

  • Incandescent bulb price: Rs. 40-80, lifespan: 1,000 hours
  • CFL bulb price: Rs. 150-300, lifespan: 8,000 hours
  • LED bulb price: Rs. 250-500, lifespan: 25,000 hours

For 10 bulbs at Rs. 350 each, total investment is Rs. 3,500. With monthly savings of Rs. 3,856 vs incandescent, payback happens in less than one month. With savings of Rs. 378 vs CFL, payback takes about 9 months.

Annual Savings Summary

Over one year, a 10-bulb household running 6 hours daily saves:

  • Vs incandescent: Rs. 46,272 per year
  • Vs CFL: Rs. 4,536 per year

Since LEDs last 25,000 hours (over 11 years at 6 hours/day), you will not need replacements for more than a decade. This makes LEDs the clear winner for both monthly savings and long-term value.

Other Benefits of LEDs

Beyond power savings, LEDs offer other advantages:

  • Instant brightness: No warm-up time like CFLs
  • Cooler operation: Less heat means lower AC load in summer
  • Durable: No fragile glass or filaments
  • Better dimming: Many LEDs support smooth dimming

For the best results, look for LED bulbs with a high lm/W rating and a warm white color temperature (3000K-4000K) for home use.

FAQs

Are LED bulbs really worth the higher price?

Yes. LEDs pay for themselves within a few months through lower electricity bills and last up to 25,000 hours, which is over a decade of typical use.

How much can I save by switching one bulb?

Replacing one 60W incandescent with a 9W LED saves about Rs. 385 per month at average NEPRA rates. With 10 bulbs, savings exceed Rs. 3,800 monthly.

Do LEDs work with existing fittings?

Yes. Most LEDs use standard E27 or B22 bases that fit existing home fittings in Pakistan. Always check the base type before buying.

Which color temperature is best for home?

Warm white (3000-4000K) is recommended for living rooms and bedrooms. Cool white (5000K+) works better for kitchens and study areas.

Can LEDs help reduce AC costs too?

Yes. LEDs produce very little heat compared to incandescent bulbs, which reduces the cooling load on your AC in summer months.
