LESCO Electricity Bill Calculator 2026 - Lahore Tariff Guide

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

LESCO serves one of the most populated regions of Pakistan, so understanding your LESCO bill is essential. This guide explains LESCO coverage, how to check your bill online, and how to estimate your monthly charges using NEPRA 2026 slab rates. You will also find tips to reduce your bill and links to tools that can help you plan your monthly usage.

If you want a quick estimate right away, use the LESCO bill calculator. You can also use the main calculator if you want to compare multiple DISCOs or consumer types.

LESCO coverage areas

LESCO (Lahore Electric Supply Company) covers Lahore, Kasur, Sheikhupura, Okara, and nearby districts. If your bill shows LESCO as the DISCO, you can use the LESCO calculator or any of our NEPRA based tools. The tariff structure is aligned with national slabs, so your bill depends on the number of units you consume each month.

How LESCO bills are calculated

LESCO bills follow NEPRA 2026 slab rates. The first step is to identify your consumer type: lifeline, protected, or non-protected. Then, your units are split into slabs and charged at different rates. After that, fixed charges, GST, income tax, and surcharges like the Neelum Jhelum charge are added. The FCA line may increase or reduce your bill depending on the month. If you want a deeper explanation, read how electricity bills are calculated.

How to check LESCO bill online

The easiest method is the PITC online portal. Open the website, enter your 14 digit reference number, and view the current bill. You can also download a PDF for printing. For a step by step walkthrough, see our guide on checking WAPDA bills online. Some users also use the LESCO mobile app or SMS services, but the PITC portal remains the most reliable option.

Common reasons for high LESCO bills

The most common reason is a slab jump. If your household crosses 200 or 300 units, the per-unit rate increases, which raises the total. Another reason is seasonal usage, especially in summer when air conditioning and cooling fans run longer. FCA changes can also raise the total even if units are similar. Finally, non-filer income tax can add a significant amount to the bill.

To understand the rate jump, check the slab table in the tariff slab guide. Use the calculator to see how many units push you into a higher bracket.

Tips to reduce your LESCO bill

Start by tracking your units. If you are close to a slab limit, reduce consumption during the rest of the month. Use efficient appliances, reduce standby power, and shift heavy loads to off-peak hours. If your roof allows it, explore net metering and rooftop solar. The solar savings calculator can help you estimate potential payback.

LESCO helpline and complaint tips

For billing issues, you can call LESCO helplines or visit the nearest customer service office. If you suspect a faulty meter or incorrect reading, file a complaint quickly and keep a copy of your request. Our complaint guide lists the steps and what documents to keep.

FAQs

Which areas are covered by LESCO?

LESCO covers Lahore, Kasur, Sheikhupura, Okara, and nearby districts.

How can I check my LESCO bill online?

Use the PITC portal and enter your reference number to view or download the bill.

Are LESCO rates different from NEPRA slabs?

LESCO generally follows NEPRA national slabs with standard surcharges.

Why is my LESCO bill high in summer?

Higher usage pushes you into a higher slab and increases taxes and surcharges.

How can I reduce my LESCO bill?

Track units, reduce peak usage, and consider solar for long term savings.
