Net Metering Pakistan 2026: How to Sell Solar Power Back to DISCO

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

Net metering allows Pakistani households to export excess solar power to the grid and receive credits on their electricity bills. This makes solar much more attractive because you can offset your bill even when you generate more than you use. This guide explains how net metering works, who is eligible, and how to apply in 2026.

If you are still evaluating solar, start with the solar savings calculator and read our solar cost vs savings analysis.

What is net metering?

Net metering lets you send excess solar units to the grid and receive credit for those units. If your household uses 300 units and your solar system produces 350 units, the extra 50 units are credited and reduce your bill. In some months, the credit can carry forward depending on the DISCO policy.

Eligibility requirements

Eligibility can vary by DISCO, but most require a grid connected solar system, a net meter, and proper approvals. You may need a minimum system size and a licensed installer. Always check your DISCO requirements or ask your installer for the latest documents.

Application process (overview)

The typical process includes:

  1. Install a grid connected solar system with a certified installer.
  2. Apply to your DISCO with required documents.
  3. DISCO inspection and approval.
  4. Install net meter and sign the agreement.

Processing time varies by DISCO. Keep copies of your application and follow up regularly if delays occur.

How net metering affects your bill

Net metering reduces your billed units, which can keep you in lower slabs. This multiplies your savings because you avoid both high per-unit rates and some taxes that depend on energy charges. Use the main bill calculator to see how lower units reduce the total, then compare with your solar estimate.

Which DISCOs allow net metering?

Most major DISCOs support net metering, including LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, FESCO, and K-Electric. Requirements and timelines can differ, so verify with your DISCO or installer. If you are in Lahore, use the LESCO calculator and then compare solar savings for a complete view.

Tips for a smooth approval

Work with an experienced installer, ensure paperwork is complete, and keep copies of all documents. If you face delays, follow up with the DISCO office in writing. Having a clear system layout and inspection report can speed up the process.

FAQs

What is net metering?

Net metering lets you export excess solar units to the grid and receive credits.

Who is eligible for net metering in Pakistan?

Most consumers with a solar system and a compatible meter can apply through their DISCO.

How long does net metering approval take?

Approval timelines vary by DISCO and can take several weeks.

Does net metering reduce my bill?

Yes, exported units are credited against your consumption, reducing net units billed.

Do all DISCOs offer net metering?

Most major DISCOs support net metering, but requirements can differ.
