What is NJ Surcharge in Electricity Bill? Neelum Jhelum Surcharge Explained

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

The NJ surcharge is a small but regular charge on Pakistan electricity bills. It is linked to the Neelum Jhelum hydropower project and is collected as a per-unit surcharge. Even though it looks minor, it contributes to the final bill total, especially for households that consume several hundred units each month.

In 2026, the reference charge used for estimates is about Rs 0.10 per unit. If you consume 400 units, the NJ surcharge adds roughly Rs 40. It is not the biggest line item, but understanding it helps you read your bill confidently and estimate accurately.

Our Pakistan electricity bill calculator automatically includes this surcharge in the breakdown. You can see it clearly when you calculate your bill for LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, or any other DISCO.

Why the NJ surcharge exists

The Neelum Jhelum hydropower project is one of Pakistan largest power infrastructure developments, built with substantial investment. To recover a portion of the costs and keep the project financially sustainable, NEPRA approved this surcharge as a separate line item. The idea is that all consumers who benefit from the national grid contribute proportionally to the cost of this project. That is why the surcharge is applied per unit rather than as a flat amount.

The surcharge has been part of Pakistani electricity bills for many years. Its rate can change based on NEPRA decisions and project cost updates. In 2026, the rate used in estimates is Rs 0.10 per unit. This is a small amount, but it appears consistently every month, which makes it a predictable part of your bill. Unlike FCA, it does not fluctuate wildly based on fuel prices.

How it is calculated

The calculation is simple: NJ surcharge per unit multiplied by the number of units you consumed. For example, if the surcharge is Rs 0.10 per unit and you consumed 300 units, the NJ surcharge is Rs 30. This amount appears as a separate line item or part of the surcharge section on your bill. The calculator on this site includes the NJ surcharge automatically in the breakdown if you use it for any DISCO, including LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, or K-Electric.

Because the calculation is linear, higher usage households pay more in absolute terms. A home using 600 units would pay Rs 60 in NJ surcharge. While this is not a large amount compared to energy charges or GST, it adds to the overall bill and should be accounted for when estimating your monthly total. For the most accurate estimate, always include all surcharges listed in the calculator.

Does it apply to all consumers?

In most cases, yes. The NJ surcharge is applied across consumer categories. There may be specific exemptions from time to time due to policy changes, but as a rule it is applied to both protected and non-protected households. It does not replace other charges like FCA or GST. It is an additional item on top of standard charges. Lifeline consumers may be exempt in some periods, so check your latest bill to confirm.

This surcharge is also applied to commercial and industrial connections in most DISCO regions. The rate may differ slightly for different consumer categories, but the principle is the same. For a typical household, the rate is consistent across all DISCOs, which means you do not need to worry about different rates when moving between cities. The calculator on this site uses a standard rate for all estimates.

How much does NJ surcharge affect a typical bill?

For smaller households, the impact is modest. A home using 150 units pays about Rs 15. A home using 400 units pays about Rs 40. The real impact is seen when combined with other surcharges such as financing cost surcharge and FCA. Together, these small charges can add up to a few hundred rupees for high usage households. If you want to see the combined surcharge total for your specific usage, try the main calculator and review the surcharge line.

For example, a 500 unit household might pay Rs 50 for NJ surcharge, Rs 215 for financing cost surcharge, and a variable FCA amount. These three together could easily add Rs 300 to Rs 500 to the monthly bill. That is more than the meter rent and TV fee combined, so it is worth understanding each of these line items when reading your bill or planning your monthly budget.

Why it still matters even though it is small

Understanding small charges like NJ surcharge helps you read your bill confidently and verify estimates. When you compare the official bill to your estimate from the calculator, these items explain why the total is higher than just energy charges and fixed charges. Knowing each line item also helps you spot missing or unusually high charges. If you see an NJ surcharge that is far above Rs 0.10 per unit, it may indicate a calculation error or a recent rate change you were not aware of.

For households on a tight monthly budget, every rupee counts. Knowing that the NJ surcharge adds about Rs 30 to Rs 60 per month helps you plan more accurately. Combined with other surcharges and taxes, the total can be estimated well in advance using the calculator on this site. This is especially useful during summer months when usage spikes and every additional line item becomes larger in absolute value.
