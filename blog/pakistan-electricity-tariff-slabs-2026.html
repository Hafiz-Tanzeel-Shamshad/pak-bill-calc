Pakistan Electricity Tariff Slabs 2026: Complete Unit Rate Chart

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

NEPRA sets national electricity tariff slabs for most households in Pakistan. These slabs define how much you pay per unit at different usage levels. The rate rises as your monthly units increase, which is why your bill can jump when you cross 200 or 300 units. This guide lists the 2026 slab rates for lifeline, protected, and non-protected consumers, plus a quick note on commercial and time-of-use tariffs.

Use this slab chart with the main bill calculator to estimate your total or with your DISCO specific page such as MEPCO. Knowing the slab rate that applies to your household helps you plan monthly usage and reduce surprises.

Commercial and time-of-use notes

Commercial tariffs can include time-of-use (ToU) rates if the sanctioned load is 5 kW or above. In that case, peak hours are charged higher than off-peak. The NEPRA 2026 reference rates are Rs 24.33 per unit for peak and Rs 18.01 per unit for off-peak. Always check your DISCO for the exact ToU schedule. For a practical household estimate, you can still use the standard slabs in our calculators.

How to use the slab chart

First, determine your consumer type. If you are protected, use the protected slab table. If your average usage exceeds 200 units, use the non-protected slabs. Then multiply the units in each slab by the slab rate and add the results. Finally, add fixed charges, GST, income tax, and surcharges. The bill calculation guide shows these steps in detail.

For a faster estimate, enter your units in the main calculator and select your consumer type. The calculator automatically shows a slab breakdown table and a visual chart so you can see which slab contributes most to your total.

Why slab rates increase with usage

NEPRA designs the tariff structure to encourage efficient electricity consumption. The first units you use each month are the cheapest because they cover essential needs like lighting, fans, and basic appliances. As you consume more, the per-unit rate increases. This progressive slab system means households that use more electricity pay a higher average rate, which helps cross-subsidize lower rates for lifeline and protected consumers. Understanding this structure is the first step to managing your bill. If you know that crossing 300 units will push part of your consumption into the 33.10 Rs per unit bracket, you can make conscious decisions about appliance usage in the last week of the billing cycle.

Impact of slab jumps on your bill

A slab jump happens when your monthly consumption moves from one slab range into the next. For example, moving from 190 units to 210 units does not just add 20 more units at a higher rate. It also means the units in the higher slab are charged at the new rate, and the overall average per-unit cost increases. This is why a small increase in usage can sometimes result in a disproportionately large increase in your total bill. If you track your monthly units and see you are close to a slab boundary, reducing usage by even 10 to 20 units in the final days of the billing cycle can save you more than the units themselves would suggest.

How FCA interacts with slabs

While the slab rates remain fixed for the tariff year, the Fuel Cost Adjustment (FCA) is recalculated every month based on actual fuel costs incurred by power generation companies. The FCA is applied uniformly per unit and is not slab-dependent. However, because your total energy charge is based on the slab rate, the FCA amount you pay will be higher if you are in a higher slab purely because you consumed more units. In months when FCA is positive, the slab jump effect becomes even more pronounced. For the latest FCA values, refer to the monthly NEPRA notifications on the official NEPRA website.

FAQs

What are NEPRA tariff slabs?

Tariff slabs are unit ranges with different per-unit rates set by NEPRA.

Do all DISCOs use the same slabs?

Most DISCOs follow NEPRA national slabs, with minor local adjustments.

Why do rates increase after 200 units?

NEPRA uses higher slabs to discourage excessive consumption.

Are commercial rates different?

Commercial tariffs can differ and may use ToU rates; check your DISCO for exact details.

How can I use the slab chart?

Track monthly units and estimate which slab your usage falls into.
