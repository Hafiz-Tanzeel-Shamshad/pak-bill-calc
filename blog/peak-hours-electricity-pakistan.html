Peak Hours Electricity Pakistan: What They Are and How to Avoid Them

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

Peak hours are time periods when electricity demand is highest. During these hours, the per-unit rate can be higher for consumers with a time-of-use (ToU) meter, especially those with a sanctioned load of 5 kW or above. Understanding peak hours helps you shift heavy usage to off-peak times and reduce your bill.

If your meter is not ToU, you still benefit from shifting usage because it helps you keep total units under slab thresholds. Use the main calculator to see how a small unit reduction can keep you in a lower slab.

What are peak hours?

Peak hours are defined by DISCOs and can change seasonally. Typically, the evening hours are considered peak because demand is highest when most households return home and switch on lights, fans, and appliances. The ToU tariff is higher in peak hours and lower in off-peak hours.

Who is affected by peak hours?

Peak hour tariffs mainly affect customers with ToU meters. These are often households or businesses with a sanctioned load of 5 kW or higher. If your bill has a separate line for peak and off-peak units, you are on a ToU tariff. Otherwise, peak hours do not change your per-unit rate, but they still affect the national demand and fuel costs, which can impact FCA.

Why peak hours increase the bill

ToU rates are higher during peak hours. For 2026, the reference rates are around Rs 24.33 per unit for peak and Rs 18.01 per unit for off-peak. If you consume a large share of your monthly units during peak hours, the total can be much higher than a flat slab estimate.

Appliances to shift to off-peak

Focus on heavy appliances that can be scheduled. Washing machines, irons, water pumps, electric ovens, and large chargers should be used during off-peak hours when possible. If you can shift only a few hours of usage, you can still see meaningful savings. The appliance cost guide helps you identify which devices matter most.

How to check your ToU schedule

Check your bill for a ToU schedule or visit your DISCO website. The schedule can change seasonally, so review it every few months. If you are unsure, call your DISCO helpline or visit a customer service center.

Peak hours and slab rates

Even if you are on a ToU tariff, slab rates still matter. Your total units determine which slab you fall into, while ToU rates determine how those units are priced. The best strategy is to reduce total units and shift heavy usage to off-peak hours. Use the slab chart to see how much a reduction can save.

FAQs

What are peak hours in Pakistan?

Peak hours are time periods when electricity demand is high and ToU rates are higher.

Do peak hours apply to all consumers?

Peak hours usually apply to ToU meters and higher load customers.

Which appliances should I move to off-peak?

Washing machines, irons, water pumps, and charging should be shifted when possible.

How much can I save by avoiding peak hours?

Savings depend on how much of your usage shifts to off-peak and your slab rates.

Where can I check my ToU schedule?

Your DISCO or bill typically lists the current ToU schedule.
