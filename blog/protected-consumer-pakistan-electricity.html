What is Protected Consumer in Pakistan Electricity? Full Explanation

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

Protected consumer status is one of the most important parts of Pakistan electricity bills. If you qualify as a protected consumer, your per-unit rate is much lower than non-protected rates. For many families, staying protected can save thousands of rupees over the summer. This guide explains what the protected category means, how it is calculated, and how to keep your household within the protected range.

The protected rule is simple: if your average monthly units over the last three months is 200 units or less, you are treated as protected. Your bill then uses the protected slab rates, which are lower than non-protected rates. This calculation is done by your DISCO every billing cycle, so the status can change when your usage changes.

To see how this affects your own bill, use the main bill calculator. Select the protected consumer option and compare it with non-protected to understand the gap. In many cases the difference is large enough that it is worth changing daily habits to stay protected.

Protected vs non-protected: the key difference

Protected consumers pay lower slab rates for the first 200 units. Non-protected consumers pay much higher rates for the same usage. This is why a household that crosses the 200 unit average threshold can see a sudden jump in the bill even if the increase in units looks small. The tariff slab chart in our 2026 slab guide shows the difference clearly.

Think of it as a safety bracket. As long as your average stays below 200, the system protects you with lower rates. Once you move above that line, the protection is removed. It is not a penalty, but the difference feels like one because the rates are so different.

How the three month average works

DISCOs look at your last three bills and calculate the average units. For example, if your last three bills show 180, 210, and 190 units, your average is 193 units and you stay protected. If the same household uses 240, 220, and 210 units, the average is 223 units and the status becomes non-protected.

This is why one high month does not always change your status immediately. The average gives you a little buffer, but it also means you need to manage usage consistently over multiple months. Many households see a shift in summer because the average rises due to cooling usage.

What counts toward the 200 unit average?

The calculation is based on the total units recorded on your bill. This includes all appliances and usage in the household, including water pumps, refrigerators, fans, and air conditioners. It does not depend on your bill amount or your load category. It is purely based on units consumed.

If you want to track your average, keep a simple record of the last three bills. Add the units and divide by three. This quick check tells you whether you are close to the protected limit. You can also use the LESCO calculator or any other DISCO calculator to estimate if your next bill might push the average higher.

How to stay protected: practical tips

Staying protected is mostly about reducing high-usage habits. The biggest drivers are air conditioning, electric heaters, and water pumps. Use efficient appliances, set AC temperatures to 26 C or higher, and avoid running multiple heavy appliances at the same time. Small changes can keep your average below the 200 unit mark.

Another approach is to track your mid-month usage. If you are already at 120 units by the middle of the month, you know you may cross 240 by the end. Use the last two weeks to reduce usage and keep your average safer. The article on reducing electricity bills has a detailed checklist.

What if you lose protected status?

If your average crosses 200 units, your bill will move to non-protected slab rates. This does not mean you can never return to protected status. As soon as your three month average falls below 200 again, you will be treated as protected. Many households move in and out of protection during the year, especially between winter and summer.

FAQs

What is a protected consumer?

A protected consumer is a household with an average of 200 units or less over the last three months.

How is protected status calculated?

Your DISCO averages your last three monthly unit consumptions.

Can I lose protected status?

Yes. If your three month average goes above 200 units, you become non-protected.

Does the rule apply to all DISCOs?

Yes. The protected consumer rule is part of NEPRA tariffs applied across DISCOs.

How can I stay protected?

Keep your monthly usage below 200 units on average by managing cooling, lighting, and standby loads.
