Solar ROI in Pakistan 2026: Complete Guide to Payback and Savings

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

What Is Solar ROI?

Solar ROI (Return on Investment) measures how quickly your solar system pays for itself through electricity savings. In Pakistan, where grid electricity costs Rs. 35-55 per unit depending on slab, solar typically pays back within 3-7 years. After that, your electricity is nearly free for the remaining 18-22+ years of system life.

This guide explains how to calculate solar ROI for your home or business, including net metering credits, maintenance costs, and the impact of NEPRA tariff increases.

Key Factors That Affect Solar ROI

Your solar payback period depends on several variables:

  • System cost: Lower panel prices mean faster payback. Current rates: Rs. 120,000-150,000 per kW for on-grid
  • Current electricity rate: Higher per-unit rates = faster payback. Households in upper slabs (>700 units at Rs. 55/unit) see the fastest returns
  • System size: Sized to match your consumption avoids waste. Oversizing adds cost without proportional savings
  • Net metering: Selling excess power to the grid improves ROI significantly. Without net metering, battery-less systems waste surplus generation
  • Battery cost: Batteries add Rs. 200,000-500,000 and extend payback by 2-3 years but provide backup during outages

How to Calculate Solar Payback

Follow these steps to calculate your solar ROI:

  1. Find your annual electricity cost: Add up your last 12 months of bills. The average Pakistani home spends Rs. 60,000-120,000 per year on electricity.
  2. Determine system size: Divide annual units by 1,400 (average kWh produced per kW per year in Pakistan). For 10,000 units/year, you need approximately 7 kW.
  3. Get system cost: 7 kW on-grid × Rs. 135,000/kW = Rs. 945,000
  4. Calculate annual savings: Same as your current annual bill if the system covers all usage = Rs. 80,000/year
  5. Payback period: Rs. 945,000 ÷ Rs. 80,000 = 11.8 years without net metering. With net metering (100% offset), payback improves to 5-7 years.

ROI With and Without Net Metering

Net metering dramatically improves solar ROI:

  • Without net metering: Solar only saves during daylight hours. Payback: 8-12 years
  • With net metering: Every unit generated offsets a grid unit. Payback: 3-7 years

Without net metering, you need batteries to use solar power at night, which adds Rs. 200,000+ to system cost. With net metering, the grid acts as your battery for free. All DISCOs in Pakistan now support net metering under NEPRA regulations.

Impact of Future Tariff Increases

NEPRA typically increases electricity tariffs by 5-15% per year. These increases make solar even more valuable over time. If tariffs rise 10% annually:

  • Year 1 savings: Rs. 80,000
  • Year 2 savings: Rs. 88,000
  • Year 5 savings: Rs. 1,17,000
  • Year 10 savings: Rs. 1,89,000

This means actual payback is often faster than simple calculations suggest because the value of the electricity you offset keeps rising. Over 25 years, even a Rs. 10 lakh system can save Rs. 40-70 lakh in cumulative electricity costs.

Is Solar Worth It for Your Home?

Solar makes financial sense if:

  • Your monthly bill is Rs. 8,000 or more (approx. 300+ units)
  • You plan to stay in your home for 5+ years
  • Your roof has space for panels (approx. 100 sq ft per kW)
  • Your DISCO supports net metering (all do)

FAQs

What is the average ROI for solar in Pakistan?

The average payback period is 4-7 years for on-grid systems with net metering. After payback, you get 18-21 years of nearly free electricity.

How do I calculate solar ROI?

Divide the total installed cost by your annual electricity savings. Example: Rs. 700,000 system saving Rs. 120,000/year = 5.8 years payback.

Does net metering improve ROI?

Yes. Net metering improves ROI by 40-60% compared to solar without net metering, because you can offset all your units — not just daytime usage.

What is the best solar ROI: on-grid, off-grid, or hybrid?

On-grid systems have the best ROI (3-7 year payback) because batteries are expensive. Hybrid systems with batteries offer backup but extend payback to 6-9 years.

How much can I save with solar over 25 years?

A 5 kW system can save Rs. 2.5-4.5 crore over 25 years depending on tariff increases and system size.
