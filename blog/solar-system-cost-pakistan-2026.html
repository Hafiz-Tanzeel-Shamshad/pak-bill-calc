Solar System Cost in Pakistan 2026: Complete Price Breakdown

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 23, 2026

Why Solar Is Growing in Pakistan

With NEPRA tariff increases pushing electricity costs higher each year, more Pakistani households and businesses are considering solar power. The upfront cost of a solar system has dropped significantly in recent years, making it more accessible than ever.

This guide breaks down the complete cost of solar systems in Pakistan for 2026, including panels, inverters, batteries, structure, and installation. Use this information to budget for your solar project and compare it with grid electricity costs.

Solar System Types and Costs

There are three main types of solar systems available in Pakistan:

  • On-Grid (Grid-Tied): No battery, works with the grid. Most affordable option. Ideal for areas with reliable grid power. Cost: Rs. 120,000-150,000 per kW
  • Off-Grid (Standalone): Includes battery bank for full independence. Required where grid is unavailable. Cost: Rs. 200,000-280,000 per kW
  • Hybrid: Combines grid connection with battery backup. Most flexible but highest cost. Cost: Rs. 250,000-350,000 per kW

These prices include panels, inverter, mounting structure, wiring, and installation. Battery costs vary by type (see below).

Solar Panel Prices (2026)

Solar panel prices in Pakistan have dropped to historic lows:

  • 550W Mono PERC (Canadian / Longi / Jinko): Rs. 22,000-27,000 per panel
  • 585W N-Type (high efficiency): Rs. 28,000-33,000 per panel
  • Local / Rebranded Panels: Rs. 16,000-20,000 per panel (shorter warranty)

For a 5 kW system, you need 9-10 panels of 550W. Panel cost alone: Rs. 2,00,000-2,50,000.

Inverter and Battery Costs

Inverter prices depend on type and capacity:

  • 5 kW On-Grid Inverter (Huawei / Growatt / Sofar): Rs. 80,000-120,000
  • 5 kW Hybrid Inverter: Rs. 130,000-200,000
  • 5 kW Off-Grid Inverter: Rs. 100,000-160,000

Battery costs (for off-grid and hybrid systems):

  • Lead-Acid 200Ah (Tubular): Rs. 28,000-35,000 each (lifespan 3-4 years)
  • Lithium-Ion 5 kWh (LFP): Rs. 180,000-250,000 (lifespan 8-10 years)
  • Lithium-Ion 10 kWh (LFP): Rs. 350,000-480,000

Complete System Cost Examples

Estimated total installed cost (2026 rates in Lahore/Karachi/Islamabad):

  • 3 kW On-Grid: Rs. 350,000-450,000
  • 5 kW On-Grid: Rs. 600,000-750,000
  • 5 kW Hybrid with 5 kWh Lithium: Rs. 1,000,000-1,300,000
  • 10 kW On-Grid: Rs. 1,200,000-1,500,000
  • 10 kW Hybrid with 10 kWh Lithium: Rs. 2,000,000-2,500,000

These are complete installed prices including structure, wiring, earthing, AC/DC boxes, and net metering application fees.

Net Metering and Payback

Net metering allows you to sell excess solar power back to the grid. DISCOs credit your account at the same per-unit rate you are charged. This means your grid bill reduces to zero or near-zero if your system is sized correctly.

Typical payback period in Pakistan:

  • 3 kW On-Grid: 3-4 years
  • 5 kW On-Grid: 4-5 years
  • 5 kW Hybrid: 5-7 years

With a 25-year panel warranty and inverter lifespan of 10-15 years, solar systems generate savings for decades after payback. Use our solar savings calculator to estimate your specific payback period.

FAQs

What is the cost of a 5 kW solar system in Pakistan in 2026?

A 5 kW on-grid solar system costs Rs. 600,000-750,000 installed. A 5 kW hybrid system with lithium battery costs Rs. 1,000,000-1,300,000.

Is net metering available for all DISCOs?

Yes, all DISCOs including LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, FESCO, GEPCO, PESCO, HESCO, SEPCO, QESCO, and K-Electric offer net metering under NEPRA regulations.

How long do solar panels last?

Premium solar panels (Canadian, Longi, Jinko) come with 25-year performance warranty. They produce at least 80% of rated power after 25 years.

Which solar system is best for home?

A 5 kW on-grid or hybrid system is ideal for a typical Pakistani home with 500-800 monthly units. See our solar calculator for a custom recommendation.

Can I run AC on solar?

Yes. Solar systems can easily run ACs. A 5 kW system can power 2-3 ACs along with other appliances during peak sunlight hours.
