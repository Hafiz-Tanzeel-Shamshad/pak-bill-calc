Why is My Electricity Bill So High in Pakistan? 8 Common Reasons

By Hafiz Tanzeel Shamshad — Updated: May 20, 2026

If your electricity bill is suddenly higher than usual, you are not alone. The most common reasons are slab jumps, FCA changes, and tax differences. This guide explains the top eight causes of high bills and what you can do to verify the charges and reduce your monthly total.

Start by checking your units and running them through the Pakistan electricity bill calculator. This gives you a clear estimate to compare with the official bill.

1. Slab jump

The biggest reason for a high bill is crossing into a higher slab. If you move from 200 to 210 units, the new units are charged at a higher rate. The impact grows as you cross 300 and 400 units. Check the slab chart to see the rate increase.

2. Higher usage in summer

Air conditioners, fans, and water pumps increase usage during hot months. Even a few extra hours of AC per day can add hundreds of units per month. This can push you into higher slabs and increase the bill quickly.

3. FCA changes

The Fuel Cost Adjustment changes monthly and can raise the bill even if your units remain the same. Some months FCA is negative, but in others it is positive. Read the FCA guide to understand how it works.

4. Non-filer income tax

If your filer status changed, your income tax line item may increase. Non-filers pay a higher rate, which can add noticeable cost. Check the income tax guide for details.

5. Wrong meter reading

Incorrect readings can push your units higher than reality. Compare the meter reading on your bill with the actual meter. If it looks wrong, file a complaint with your DISCO immediately.

6. Old or inefficient appliances

Old ACs, fridges, and pumps use more power. The appliance guide shows which devices use the most units.

7. Additional charges and surcharges

Items like NJ surcharge, financing cost, meter rent, and TV fee add to the total. These are small individually but add up together. Use a calculator breakdown to see each line.

8. Billing adjustments or arrears

Sometimes a bill includes previous arrears or adjustments. Check the bill for any extra lines or outstanding balance.

What to do next

Use the calculator to estimate your bill, check the meter reading, and review each line item. If something looks wrong, file a complaint and keep your complaint number. Our complaint guide explains the steps.

FAQs

Why did my bill jump even with the same units?

FCA adjustments or a slab change can increase the total even if units are similar.

Does non-filer tax increase my bill?

Yes, non-filer income tax is higher and increases the total.

Can a wrong meter reading cause a high bill?

Yes, incorrect readings can push you into higher slabs.

How can I verify my bill?

Use the PITC portal and compare with a calculator estimate.

What should I do if the bill is wrong?

File a complaint with your DISCO and keep the complaint number.
