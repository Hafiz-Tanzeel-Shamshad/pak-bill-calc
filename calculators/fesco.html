About FESCO Billing in 2026

FESCO (Faisalabad Electric Supply Company) covers Faisalabad, Sargodha, Jhang, Toba Tek Singh, and surrounding areas. The bill calculation follows the NEPRA 2026 slab system, so each block of units is charged at a different rate. If your household crosses a slab boundary, your total can rise quickly, even if the increase in units looks small. This is why it is important to watch monthly units, especially in summer.

This FESCO calculator starts with the energy charge from your consumer slab and then adds the standard monthly charges. These include GST, income tax based on filer status, a fixed charge based on load, and per-unit surcharges like the Neelum Jhelum and financing cost charges. The FCA option is shown as an estimated adjustment because it changes every month and is usually displayed as a separate line on your bill.

For accurate verification, compare the calculator estimate with your official bill on the PITC portal. If you believe the reading is incorrect, file a complaint quickly to avoid slab jumps on the next bill. Long term savings are possible by using efficient appliances and shifting heavy loads to off-peak hours. If you have adequate roof space, net metering can reduce your monthly bill substantially.

FESCO serves Faisalabad, Sargodha, Gujranwala, and surrounding industrial areas of Punjab. As one of Pakistan's largest industrial DISCOs, FESCO has a mix of residential and commercial consumers. Industrial areas like Faisalabad's textile mills can cause voltage fluctuations that affect home appliances. If you run a home-based business, ensure you are on the correct consumer type tariff to avoid back-billing issues.

FESCO consumers in Sargodha and Jhang sometimes face delayed bill delivery, which can lead to late payment surcharges if the bill arrives after the due date. It is recommended to register your mobile number with FESCO to receive SMS billing alerts so you know your amount before the deadline. Disputed meter readings should be reported within 10 days of bill issuance to qualify for a re-reading request without penalty.

FESCO accepts payments through the PITC online portal, JazzCash, EasyPaisa, and banking partners including HBL, UBL, National Bank, and Bank of Punjab. You can also pay via ATMs and mobile banking apps of partner banks without any additional processing fee. For textile mill workers and factory employees, FESCO has tie-ups with several industrial units to offer payroll deduction for residential bills.

Faisalabad is a major industrial hub with textile mills that create fluctuating load demands on the grid. Residential consumers near industrial zones in Samundri and Toba Tek Singh may experience voltage dips that affect appliance performance during peak factory hours. Using voltage stabilizers for air conditioners and refrigerators can help protect sensitive electronics from damage caused by sudden fluctuations in supply.

Frequently Asked Questions about FESCO Bills

How do I pay my FESCO bill?

Pay through bill.pitc.com.pk, JazzCash, EasyPaisa, or at HBL, UBL, NBP, and Bank of Punjab branches across Faisalabad, Sargodha, Jhang, and Toba Tek Singh regions.

What is the FESCO helpline number?

FESCO's helpline is 118 for complaints and billing queries. The Faisalabad headquarters office number is 041-9220321 for escalated issues and technical support during business hours.

Does FESCO support net metering?

Yes, FESCO supports net metering under the AEDB policy for both residential and commercial consumers. Applications can be submitted online via the FESCO website or at the headquarters in Faisalabad.

How can I check my FESCO bill online?

Visit bill.pitc.com.pk and enter your 14-digit reference number to view your current bill. The FESCO official website also provides a bill viewing service for registered users with payment history access.

Why is my FESCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

FCA adjustments that change every month, slab boundary crossings between 200 and 300 units, quarterly tariff surcharges, and non-filer tax differences are the most common reasons for variance from our estimate.
