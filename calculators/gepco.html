About GEPCO Billing in 2026

GEPCO (Gujranwala Electric Power Company) supplies electricity to Gujranwala, Gujrat, Sialkot, Narowal, Hafizabad, and nearby districts. The bill is calculated under the same NEPRA 2026 slab schedule used nationwide. This means every additional block of units is priced higher than the previous block, so households that cross a slab threshold see a noticeable jump in the monthly total.

The GEPCO calculator above uses the official slab rates and adds common charges such as GST, income tax based on filer status, and per-unit surcharges. It also includes fixed charges depending on your sanctioned load. The optional FCA line is shown as an estimated negative adjustment because fuel cost changes are published monthly and can lower or raise the total for that billing cycle.

If your bill looks higher than expected, review the meter reading and unit count first. You can also check if your usage shifted into a higher slab during summer. For official verification or duplicate bills, use the PITC portal or visit a GEPCO customer service center. The most reliable long term savings come from reducing consumption and exploring solar with net metering if your roof is suitable.

GEPCO serves Gujranwala, Sialkot, Gujrat, Mandi Bahauddin, and Hafizabad. This region is known for its export industry, particularly Sialkot's sports goods and surgical instruments. Many households operate small home-based businesses, which means electricity usage can cross residential slab limits quickly. GEPCO has been investing in smart metering in selected areas, which helps provide more accurate billing based on actual consumption rather than estimates.

Common billing issues reported by GEPCO consumers include estimated meter readings, particularly in rural areas around Hafizabad and Mandi Bahauddin. If your bill shows a reading that does not match your actual meter, you can request a re-reading at your local GEPCO sub-division office. Many consumers also face slab jumps during summer when air conditioners and fans push usage above 300 units, which triggers higher per-unit rates under the NEPRA progressive tariff structure.

GEPCO offers multiple payment channels for customer convenience. You can pay at designated bank branches including HBL, NBP, and UBL across the service area. Mobile wallets such as JazzCash and EasyPaisa also accept GEPCO bill payments with instant confirmation. For those who prefer digital banking, most Pakistani banks offer bill payment through their mobile apps and internet banking platforms. The PITC online portal accepts credit and debit card payments for added flexibility.

Winter usage is generally lower, though some households in colder areas use electric heaters. GEPCO has been rolling out smart meters in select urban areas of Gujranwala and Sialkot, which provide accurate real-time readings and eliminate estimated billing. If your area has smart meters installed, you can track your daily consumption online.

Frequently Asked Questions about GEPCO Bills

How do I pay my GEPCO bill?

You can pay your GEPCO bill through online banking, mobile wallets like JazzCash and EasyPaisa, or at partner bank branches including HBL, NBP, and UBL. The PITC portal also accepts card payments for instant bill settlement.

What is the GEPCO helpline number?

GEPCO's customer helpline is 118, available for billing queries, fault reporting, and connection requests. You can also visit your nearest GEPCO sub-division office for in-person assistance during business hours.

Does GEPCO support net metering?

Yes, GEPCO processes net metering applications for residential solar installations under NEPRA regulations. Contact GEPCO's renewable energy department to begin the application process with your system design documents.

How can I check my GEPCO bill online?

Visit bill.pitc.com.pk, enter your 14-digit reference number from a previous bill, and you can view, download, or print your current GEPCO bill. This service is available at no cost.

Why is my GEPCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

The calculator uses base NEPRA 2026 tariff rates only. Your actual bill may include FCA variations, QTA adjustments, arrears, or late payment surcharges that are not included in the estimate.
