About IESCO Billing in 2026

IESCO (Islamabad Electric Supply Company) supplies electricity to Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Attock, Chakwal, and Jhelum. The tariff structure for IESCO customers follows the NEPRA 2026 national slabs. That means each additional block of units is charged at a higher rate, and your total can increase significantly once you move beyond 200 or 300 units. The calculator above helps you see the real impact before the official bill arrives.

This estimate starts with the energy charge calculated from the relevant slab. Then it adds fixed charges based on your sanctioned load, GST, and income tax depending on your filer status. The tool also includes per-unit surcharges like the Neelum Jhelum and financing cost charges. You can add an estimated FCA value to reflect monthly fuel cost adjustments that appear on IESCO bills.

If your IESCO bill looks unusually high, check your meter reading and units first. A slab jump or billing error can push the total beyond your expectation. Also review if your filer status has changed, since the tax difference is meaningful. To verify the official amount, use the PITC online bill portal or visit the nearest IESCO office. For long term relief, upgrade to efficient appliances and consider solar with net metering where possible.

IESCO covers Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Attock, Jhelum, and Chakwal. Being the federal capital territory, Islamabad residents sometimes experience different billing cycles or tax treatments compared to other DISCOs. IESCO generally has lower line losses than other DISCOs, which can mean slightly more stable tariff adjustments. If you live in a high-altitude area like Murree, your winter heating usage may push you into higher slabs.

IESCO typically has lower line losses compared to other DISCOs, but consumers in Rawalpindi and surrounding areas occasionally face billing errors due to old analog meters. If you notice an unexpected jump in your bill, request a meter accuracy test at your local IESCO sub-division office within the billing cycle. The test is usually free for residential consumers and can help identify whether a faulty meter is the cause of inflated charges.

IESCO bills can be paid via the PITC web portal, 1Link and NIFT member banks, and mobile wallets including JazzCash and EasyPaisa. Residents of Islamabad can also pay through designated HBL and UBL branches with instant receipt generation. For government employees living in official accommodations, IESCO offers a special salary deduction facility that ensures bills are paid directly from payroll each month.

Unlike most DISCOs, IESCO consumers in colder areas like Murree and Rawalpindi see higher winter bills due to heating appliances. Geysers, space heaters, and electric blankets can push usage over 400 units in December and January, leading to higher slab charges. Using gas geysers where available and setting electric heaters on timers can help keep winter electricity costs more manageable without sacrificing comfort.

Frequently Asked Questions about IESCO Bills

How do I pay my IESCO bill?

You can pay online via bill.pitc.com.pk, through JazzCash and EasyPaisa mobile wallets, or at any HBL, UBL, or NBP branch across Islamabad, Rawalpindi, Attock, and Jhelum.

What is the IESCO helpline number?

IESCO's customer service helpline is 118 available for complaints and queries. You can also contact the Islamabad headquarters at 051-9252232 for billing issues and technical assistance.

Does IESCO support net metering?

Yes, IESCO actively supports net metering under the national renewable energy policy. Applications can be filed through the IESCO online portal or the AEDB website for solar interconnection approval.

How can I check my IESCO bill online?

Go to bill.pitc.com.pk, input your 14-digit reference ID printed on your bill, and access your detailed monthly statement. The IESCO website also provides a bill checker tool for registered consumers.

Why is my IESCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

Differences arise from monthly FCA changes, slab transitions when crossing unit thresholds, quarterly tariff adjustments, and your filer or non-filer tax status. IESCO also applies a monthly TV license fee.
