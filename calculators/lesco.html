About LESCO Billing in 2026

LESCO (Lahore Electric Supply Company) serves Lahore, Kasur, Sheikhupura, Okara, Nankana Sahib, and surrounding areas. Like all DISCOs, it follows the national NEPRA tariff structure, which means your bill is based on a slab system. Each slab has a per-unit rate, and the rate increases as your monthly consumption goes up. This is why a small increase in units near a slab edge can make your total jump more than expected.

Our LESCO bill calculator uses the 2026 NEPRA base tariff rates and applies common monthly charges to provide a realistic estimate. We include GST, an estimated income tax based on filer status, and per-unit surcharges such as the Neelum Jhelum and financing cost charges. The FCA option is shown as an estimated negative adjustment because it changes every month. This makes the tool useful for comparing months, planning household budgets, and spotting unusual spikes before the official bill arrives.

If you notice a bill that is far higher than your estimate, check your meter reading first. A wrong reading or a slab jump is a common reason for sudden increases. You can also review whether your filer status has changed, because non-filer tax is higher. If you need your actual bill or a duplicate copy, use the PITC portal linked above or visit your local LESCO customer service center. For long term savings, improve appliance efficiency and explore net metering if your roof is suitable for solar.

For LESCO customers in Lahore, Kasur, and Sheikhupura, the peak summer months of May through August typically see the highest bills due to increased AC and fan usage. If your bill seems unusually high, cross-check your meter reading with the one printed on your bill. Many LESCO complaints stem from incorrect meter readings or estimation by the meter reader. You can report a faulty meter or disputed reading at your nearest LESCO zonal office or through the LESCO online complaint portal.

LESCO customers in Lahore often face billing issues related to estimated meter readings, especially in newer housing societies where meters are not always accessible. Disputed bills should be reported to the LESCO zonal office within 10 days of receipt to avoid late payment surcharges. Taking a photo of your meter on the bill reading date is a simple way to keep a record for comparison.

You can pay your LESCO bill through multiple channels including the LESCO website, JazzCash, EasyPaisa, 1Link member banks, and designated branches of HBL, UBL, and Allied Bank. The online payment portal also accepts debit and credit cards for convenience. Many customers find the auto-pay facility through their banking app to be the most hassle-free option to avoid missing due dates.

Summer months from May to August see LESCO bills double or even triple due to extensive AC and fan usage. Lahore's extreme heat pushes many households beyond 500 units, where the per-unit rate climbs significantly into higher slabs. Frequently Asked Questions about LESCO Bills

How do I pay my LESCO bill?

You can pay your LESCO bill online via the PITC portal, through mobile wallets like JazzCash and EasyPaisa, or at any designated bank branch including HBL and UBL across Lahore, Kasur, Sheikhupura, and surrounding areas.

What is the LESCO helpline number?

LESCO's customer helpline is 118, available 24/7 for complaints, billing queries, and fault reporting across Lahore and surrounding regions. You can also visit the LESCO headquarters on Jail Road for in-person assistance.

Does LESCO support net metering?

Yes, LESCO supports net metering under the Alternative and Renewable Energy policy of Pakistan. You can apply through the LESCO website or the AEDB portal for grid interconnection after installing solar panels.

How can I check my LESCO bill online?

Visit bill.pitc.com.pk, enter your 14-digit reference number printed on your bill, and view or download a duplicate copy. You can also use the LESCO mobile app available for Android and iOS devices.

Why is my LESCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

Differences occur due to monthly FCA adjustments, quarterly tariff adjustments, slab boundary crossings, incorrect meter readings, or changes in your filer status. Always cross-check your recorded units against your meter reading first.
