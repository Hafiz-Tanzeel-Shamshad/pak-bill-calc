About MEPCO Billing in 2026

MEPCO (Multan Electric Power Company) serves a large portion of South Punjab, including Multan, Bahawalpur, Dera Ghazi Khan, Muzaffargarh, Rahim Yar Khan, Vehari, Khanewal, and Lodhran. Consumers in these cities follow the same NEPRA tariff schedule used across Pakistan, which means your monthly charges depend on the slab you fall into. A family using 180 units will pay a different per-unit rate than a family using 280 units even if their appliances are similar.

This MEPCO calculator combines the 2026 NEPRA base tariff with typical charges applied on household bills. It adds GST, income tax based on filer status, per-unit surcharges, and fixed charges tied to your sanctioned load. The FCA toggle allows you to include a recent negative adjustment so the estimate is closer to current bills. This helps you compare months, check if a sudden jump is realistic, and plan your monthly spending.

If your official bill is much higher than expected, first verify your meter reading and look at the number of units recorded. A wrong reading can place you in a higher slab. Also check your filer status because non-filer tax can add a noticeable increase. For the latest bill or duplicate copy, use the PITC portal, the MEPCO mobile app, or your local customer service office. If you want to reduce long term costs, consider energy efficient appliances or explore net metering and rooftop solar.

MEPCO serves a large region covering Multan, Bahawalpur, Dera Ghazi Khan, and Sahiwal divisions. This area experiences extreme summer temperatures, often exceeding 45°C, which drives up cooling costs significantly. If you live in South Punjab, check whether your area falls under the protected consumer category based on your average monthly usage over the last three months. Staying within the 200-unit threshold can substantially lower your per-unit rate.

MEPCO consumers in South Punjab frequently report disputes over meter readings during summer when usage spikes dramatically. The extreme heat in cities like Multan and Bahawalpur can cause meters to malfunction, leading to inflated bills. It is advisable to take a photo of your meter reading every month on the billing date and keep a log to compare against the units printed on your bill.

MEPCO accepts payments through online banking via the PITC portal, mobile wallets such as JazzCash and EasyPaisa, and over-the-counter at National Bank, HBL, UBL, and other partner banks. You can also set up auto-pay through most banking apps to ensure you never miss a due date. For rural areas near Muzaffargarh and Rajanpur, MEPCO has established payment collection points at union council offices.

With temperatures regularly exceeding 45°C from May to July, MEPCO consumers see their highest bills in these months. Air conditioning alone can account for 60 percent of total unit consumption. Using energy-efficient inverter air conditioners and setting thermostats to 24°C rather than 18°C can reduce cooling costs by a noticeable margin during the harshest summer weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions about MEPCO Bills

How do I pay my MEPCO bill?

Pay online through the PITC bill portal, via JazzCash or EasyPaisa mobile apps, or at any NBP, HBL, or UBL branch across Multan, Bahawalpur, Dera Ghazi Khan, and South Punjab regions.

What is the MEPCO helpline number?

MEPCO's helpline is 118, reachable 24/7 for complaints, queries, and fault reporting. You can also call the Multan central office at 061-9220222 for billing disputes and technical support.

Does MEPCO support net metering?

Yes, MEPCO offers net metering for solar users across its region. Applications are submitted online or at the MEPCO headquarters in Multan under the AEDB framework for residential and commercial systems.

How can I check my MEPCO bill online?

Visit bill.pitc.com.pk, enter your 14-digit reference number, and download a PDF copy of your bill. The MEPCO mobile app also provides bill viewing, payment history, and complaint tracking features.

Why is my MEPCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

Monthly FCA variations, slab jumps when crossing 200 or 300 units, quarterly tariff adjustments, and non-filer income tax rates can all cause your actual bill to differ from the estimate provided here.
