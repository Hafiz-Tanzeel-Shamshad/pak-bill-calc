About PESCO Billing in 2026

PESCO (Peshawar Electric Supply Company) serves Peshawar, Mardan, Swat, Abbottabad, Bannu, and large parts of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The tariff structure is defined by NEPRA and uses slabs that rise as consumption grows. That means the first 100 or 200 units are cheaper than the next 100 units, which is why PESCO households benefit from keeping usage under key thresholds.

This calculator applies the 2026 NEPRA slabs and adds typical charges seen on monthly bills. The estimate includes GST, income tax based on filer status, fixed charges according to sanctioned load, and per-unit surcharges such as the Neelum Jhelum and financing cost charges. The FCA toggle reflects a recent negative adjustment, but the actual FCA can change every month.

If your bill is unexpectedly high, verify the unit reading and check whether a slab jump occurred. Seasonal usage and cooling can quickly push you into a higher bracket. For official verification or duplicate bills, use the PITC portal or a local PESCO office. For longer term relief, invest in efficient appliances, reduce standby power waste, and consider rooftop solar if your home has adequate space.

PESCO serves Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, including Peshawar, Mardan, Abbottabad, Swat, and Kohat. The varied topography from hot plains to cold mountains means electricity usage patterns differ significantly by region. In summer, air conditioning drives up usage in Peshawar and Mardan, while winter sees increased heating loads in Abbottabad and Swat. PESCO has faced challenges with line losses and load shedding in some areas.

Billing issues in the PESCO region often arise from estimated meter readings, especially in remote areas of Swat, Bannu, and Kohat where physical meter access is difficult. Consumers who receive estimated bills should request an actual reading by contacting their local PESCO sub-division office. Another common problem is slab jumps during summer when air conditioners in Peshawar and Mardan push household consumption beyond the 300-unit threshold, resulting in significantly higher per-unit rates.

PESCO customers can pay their bills through multiple convenient methods. Bank branches including HBL, NBP, and ABL accept cash payments across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Digital options such as JazzCash, EasyPaisa, and mobile banking apps are widely used. The PITC portal allows online payment via credit or debit card with instant receipt generation. PESCO also has designated collection points at its customer service centers for those who prefer paying in person.

In contrast, Abbottabad and Swat experience cold winters where electric heaters and geysers increase consumption. PESCO has been deploying smart meters in urban Peshawar and Abbottabad to improve billing accuracy and reduce complaints related to estimated readings.

Frequently Asked Questions about PESCO Bills

How do I pay my PESCO bill?

You can pay your PESCO bill through online banking, JazzCash, EasyPaisa, or at HBL, NBP, and ABL branches across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The PITC portal also supports credit and debit card payments.

What is the PESCO helpline number?

PESCO's helpline is 118 for billing complaints and fault reporting. Regional offices in Peshawar, Mardan, Abbottabad, and Swat provide walk-in assistance during working hours for complex issues.

Does PESCO support net metering?

Yes, PESCO accepts net metering applications for rooftop solar installations. Submit your system design, proof of ownership, and processing fee at your local PESCO office to begin the approval process.

How can I check my PESCO bill online?

Go to bill.pitc.com.pk, enter your 14-digit reference number, and you can view or download your PESCO bill instantly. No registration or login is required to access your bill.

Why is my PESCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

The calculator estimates the base NEPRA 2026 tariff. Your actual bill may include FCA changes, QTA adjustments, arrears, or late payment surcharges not accounted for in the estimate.
