About QESCO Billing in 2026

QESCO (Quetta Electric Supply Company) serves Quetta, Turbat, Khuzdar, and a wide area across Balochistan. The billing structure follows the NEPRA 2026 slab system. Each slab has its own rate, and the rate rises with higher monthly usage. This is why a household that crosses a slab boundary can see a noticeable increase in the total bill even if usage only increased slightly.

The calculator above applies the official slab rates and then adds typical monthly charges that appear on QESCO bills. It includes GST, income tax based on filer status, fixed charges by sanctioned load, and per-unit surcharges like the Neelum Jhelum and financing cost charges. The FCA toggle reflects an estimated fuel adjustment that changes each month and can increase or reduce the final amount.

If your bill is higher than expected, verify your meter reading first. A slab jump or reading error can make your bill appear unusually high. For official verification and duplicate bills, use the PITC portal or your nearest QESCO customer service office. Long term savings usually come from efficient appliances, careful usage in summer, and solar where feasible.

QESCO serves Quetta, Gwadar, Khuzdar, Turbat, and the rest of Balochistan. Being the largest DISCO by geographic area but with the lowest population density, QESCO faces unique infrastructure challenges. Gwadar's growing port activity is bringing new residential and commercial developments that increase electricity demand. Balochistan experiences cold winters and hot summers, so both heating and cooling costs can be significant depending on the season.

Understanding your QESCO consumer category is the first step toward an accurate bill estimate. Lifeline users who consume fewer than 50 units per month pay the lowest subsidized rates, while protected consumers using up to 200 units benefit from moderate tariffs. Non-protected residential and commercial users face progressively higher per-unit charges that increase with each slab, which means that even a small jump in monthly usage can push your bill into a noticeably higher bracket. Checking your consumer type on your latest bill helps you select the right option in the calculator above.

The QESCO billing cycle runs monthly, with bills generated from actual meter readings taken by field staff across Balochistan's vast territory. In remote areas where meter access is difficult, readings may be estimated, which can create discrepancies between your actual usage and the billed amount. If you suspect your bill is based on an estimated reading, you can request a re-reading by submitting a complaint at your nearest QESCO zonal office. The process typically takes one billing cycle to resolve, and any overpayment is adjusted in the following month's bill.

Seasonal variation significantly affects QESCO electricity consumption across Balochistan. Summer months from May to September see peak demand for air conditioning and evaporative coolers, while winter temperatures in Quetta and northern Balochistan require electric heaters and geysers that draw substantial power. By comparing your bills across different seasons, you can identify which months drive the highest consumption and plan appliance upgrades or solar investments accordingly. Many households in Balochistan are now exploring solar solutions to offset summer air conditioning costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pay my QESCO bill?

You can pay your QESCO bill through JazzCash, Easypaisa, or any bank branch. Online payment is also available via the PITC portal using your 14-digit reference number printed on your bill.

What is the QESCO helpline number?

QESCO customer support can be reached at 081-9201120. For complaints and inquiries, you can also visit your nearest QESCO zonal office in Quetta, Turbat, or Gwadar during business hours.

Does QESCO support net metering?

Yes, QESCO accepts net metering applications under NEPRA's regulations. Consumers with rooftop solar systems can apply to export surplus electricity to the grid and earn bill credits.

How can I check my QESCO bill online?

Visit bill.pitc.com.pk and enter your 14-digit reference number to view and print your current QESCO bill. The PITC portal also stores past bills for reference and duplicate printing.

Why is my QESCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

Differences occur due to FCA adjustments, QTA changes, late payment surcharges, or estimated meter readings. The calculator uses NEPRA base rates, which do not include monthly FCA fluctuations.

This calculator provides an estimated electricity bill based on NEPRA's 2026 base tariff rates. Your actual bill may differ due to monthly Fuel Cost Adjustment (FCA), Quarterly Tariff Adjustment (QTA), local surcharges, and meter reading variations.
