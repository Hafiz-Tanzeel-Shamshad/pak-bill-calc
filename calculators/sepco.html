About SEPCO Billing in 2026

SEPCO (Sukkur Electric Power Company) supplies electricity to Sukkur, Khairpur, Ghotki, Shikarpur, and nearby districts. The tariff system follows NEPRA 2026 slabs, so the per-unit rate rises as monthly usage increases. This is especially visible during summer when cooling usage pushes households into higher brackets.

The SEPCO calculator above uses the same slab rates and applies common monthly charges, including GST, income tax by filer status, fixed charges based on sanctioned load, and per-unit surcharges. The FCA toggle is included because fuel cost adjustments vary by month and can lower or raise the final bill amount.

If your official bill is much higher than your estimate, check the meter reading and unit count first. A slab jump is a common reason for unexpected totals. For verification, use the PITC portal or a local SEPCO office. To reduce long term costs, focus on efficient appliances, limit peak usage, and evaluate rooftop solar and net metering if you have sufficient space.

Common billing problems in the SEPCO region include estimated meter readings in rural areas of Ghotki, Shikarpur, and Kashmore where access during floods is difficult. Consumers who receive an estimated bill can contact their local SEPCO sub-division to request an actual reading. Another frequent issue is slab jumps during the intense summer months when air conditioning usage pushes consumption past the 300 and 500 unit thresholds, leading to steep increases in the per-unit rate.

SEPCO provides several convenient payment methods for its customers across northern Sindh. Bank branches including HBL, NBP, and UBL accept bill payments at locations in Sukkur, Larkana, Jacobabad, and Khairpur. Digital options such as JazzCash, EasyPaisa, and mobile banking apps offer a quick way to pay without visiting a branch. The PITC online portal also supports credit and debit card payments, making it possible to settle bills from anywhere with an internet connection.

Frequently Asked Questions about SEPCO Bills

How do I pay my SEPCO bill?

You can pay your SEPCO bill through online banking, mobile wallets like JazzCash and EasyPaisa, or at HBL, NBP, and UBL branches across northern Sindh. The PITC portal also accepts card payments.

What is the SEPCO helpline number?

SEPCO's helpline is 118 for billing queries and fault reporting. Regional offices in Sukkur, Larkana, Jacobabad, and Khairpur are available for walk-in assistance during business hours.

Does SEPCO support net metering?

Yes, SEPCO accepts net metering applications for rooftop solar under NEPRA regulations. Contact your nearest SEPCO office with system designs and property documents to start the process.

How can I check my SEPCO bill online?

Go to bill.pitc.com.pk, enter your 14-digit reference number, and you can view or print your SEPCO bill instantly. The portal requires no registration to access your billing information.

Why is my SEPCO bill higher than the calculator estimate?

The calculator uses base NEPRA 2026 tariff rates. Your actual SEPCO bill may include FCA variations, QTA adjustments, arrears, or late payment penalties not factored into the estimate.
