How We Estimate Solar Savings

This calculator uses practical assumptions commonly used in Pakistan. We estimate that 1 kW of solar produces about 120 units per month and needs roughly 100 square feet of roof space. The system cost estimate uses an average market price for 2026, and monthly savings are capped at your current bill to avoid overestimation. Actual performance will vary based on roof angle, sunlight hours, and equipment quality.

The payback period is calculated by dividing the estimated system cost by yearly savings. If you plan to use net metering, your savings can be higher because excess units are credited. If you live in a DISCO area with net metering support, you may recover your investment faster. Use this estimate as a starting point and request quotes from multiple vendors before making a purchase.

For bill planning, compare this estimate with your current slab rates. Solar helps the most when your household consumes more than 200 units per month, because the higher slabs are expensive. A smaller system can still reduce peak slab usage and bring your monthly bill down to a safer range. If you are not sure about your unit usage, check your last three electricity bills or use our main calculator on the homepage.

We recommend getting at least three quotes from different solar installers before committing to a system. Compare panel warranties, inverter quality, and installation service. Many DISCOs now accept net metering applications, which can significantly reduce payback time by letting you sell excess units back to the grid. Check with your local DISCO office for the latest net metering policy in your area.

For official net metering regulations and solar policy guidelines, visit the NEPRA website. You can also check AEDB (Alternate Energy Development Board) for Pakistan's renewable energy policies. This estimate is based on average market prices and typical solar production assumptions for Pakistan.

The performance of a rooftop solar system depends on several factors that vary across Pakistan's different regions. Sunlight hours differ between northern and southern cities, with Karachi and Quetta receiving more annual irradiance than Islamabad or Peshawar. Roof orientation also matters, south-facing roofs typically produce the most energy in Pakistan because the sun tracks across the southern sky. Shading from nearby buildings, trees, or water tanks can reduce panel output significantly, so a site survey is essential before finalizing your system design and panel placement.

Financial considerations go beyond the initial system cost when evaluating solar for your home. Panel degradation rates mean that a typical monocrystalline panel loses about 0.5 percent efficiency each year, so a 25-year-old system still produces roughly 87 percent of its original output. Inverter replacement is usually needed after 10 to 12 years and should be factored into your long-term savings calculation. Many solar installers in Pakistan offer financing plans that allow you to pay in installments, making the upfront investment more manageable for households that want to reduce their monthly electricity bills.

Net metering approval timelines have improved across Pakistan, but the process still requires patience. After installing your solar system, your DISCO needs to inspect and install a bidirectional meter before net metering can begin. This process typically takes four to eight weeks from application submission. Once approved, any excess units your system generates during the day are exported to the grid and credited at the applicable tariff rate, which you can use against your nighttime or low-sun consumption. Net metering credits roll over monthly and are adjusted annually.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size solar system do I need for my home?

A household using 400 units per month typically needs a 3 to 4 kW system requiring 300 to 400 square feet of roof space. Use our solar calculator above for a personalized estimate.

Is net metering available in Pakistan in 2026?

Yes, net metering is available across all major DISCOs including LESCO, MEPCO, IESCO, and K-Electric. The policy lets you export surplus solar power and receive credits on your bill.

How much roof space is needed for solar panels?

A 1 kW system needs about 100 square feet of shadow-free roof space. A 5 kW system requires around 500 square feet. Actual needs vary by panel efficiency and roof angle.

What is the payback period for rooftop solar?

Payback in Pakistan typically ranges from 3 to 6 years depending on your consumption, system size, and net metering approval. Higher monthly bills lead to faster payback.

Does solar work during load shedding?

Solar with battery storage powers your home during outages. Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off for safety. A hybrid inverter with backup ensures power during load shedding.
